About this Database

This dataset consists of average math and verbal SAT score acrosss the United States collected from the year 2005. This data is broken down into various different catagories, including, family income, gender, state, average GPA by subject, and average letter grade.

For each state, the total number of test takers and the average math and verbal scores state wide. Then the average GPA per subject is listed, along with the average number of years that students took the course. The subjects include Arts/Music, English, Forgien Language, Mathematics, Natural Science, and Social Sciences/History. The next set of data respreseted is the family income. The family income is divided into different ranges and within each range is the number of test takers in that income bracket and the average math and verbal scores.

After this is the GPA, represented as the average letter grade. The grades range from A+ to D or lower to no response (no response being that the student did not provide thier average letter grade). Within each letter grade is the average math and verbal scores as well as the total number of test takers. In addition, the is divided up by gender. For both male and female, the average math and verbal scores are listed as well as the total number of test takeres per gender. Finally, the data set has the number of males and females that scores within six different ranges for both math and verbal.
